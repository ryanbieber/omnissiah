# House Maintenance Skills

## Overview
Comprehensive home maintenance covering HVAC, plumbing, electrical, structural, and appliance systems.

---

## 1. HVAC System Maintenance

### Air Filter Replacement
- **Interval:** Every 1-3 months (more frequently in dusty environments)
- **Size:** Common sizes: 16x25x1, 16x25x4, 20x25x1 (check furnace)
- **MERV Rating:** 8-13 for residential (higher = more filtering, more restriction)
- **Cost:** $10-40 per filter
- **Procedure:**
  1. Turn off HVAC system
  2. Locate filter (usually near furnace or return air duct)
  3. Note arrow direction on frame
  4. Remove old filter, discard
  5. Insert new filter with arrow pointing toward furnace

### Annual HVAC Service
- **Timing:** Spring (AC prep) and Fall (heating prep)
- **Professional Service:** Annually by licensed technician
- **Tasks:**
  - Clean evaporator coil
  - Check refrigerant levels
  - Inspect blower components
  - Test electrical connections
  - Clean condenser coils
- **Cost:** $100-250 per visit

### Ductwork Inspection
- **Frequency:** Every 3-5 years
- **Signs:** Musty odors, uneven temperature, dust around vents
- **Cleaning:** Professional duct cleaning every 3-5 years
- **Sealing:** Use mastic sealant for leaks (not duct tape)

### Thermostat Programming
- **Heating Season:** Program lower temps when away/sleeping (68-70°F daytime)
- **Cooling Season:** Program higher temps when away (76-78°F daytime)
- **Savings:** ~1% per degree F per 8 hours of adjustment
- **Maintenance:** Replace batteries annually (spring forward, fall back)

## 2. Plumbing System Maintenance

### Leak Detection
- **Regular Inspection:** Check under sinks, around water heater
- **Water Meter Test:** Turn off all water, check meter - if moving, leak exists
- **Signs:** Stains, mold, soft drywall, unusual water bills
- **Common Locations:** Faucets, toilet tanks, water supply lines

### Faucet & Fixture Repairs
- **Dripping Faucet:** 
  - Turn off water supply
  - Disassemble handle (may need Allen wrench)
  - Replace seals/washers (O-rings, seat washers)
  - Reassemble
  - Cost: $5-50 in parts
- **Running Toilet:** 
  - Check flapper valve (replace if worn)
  - Adjust float if tank won't fill properly
  - Cost: $5-30 for repairs

### Clogged Drains
- **Prevention:** Use drain screens, avoid grease
- **Minor Clogs:** Plunger (sealed plunger for sinks/tubs)
- **Stubborn Clogs:** 
  - Plumbing snake (manual or powered)
  - Chemical drain cleaner (last resort - toxic)
- **Maintenance:** Monthly baking soda + vinegar flush

### Water Heater Maintenance
- **Type:** Tank (40-50 gal) or Tankless
- **Tank Water Heater:**
  - Flush sediment annually (turn off, drain 5 gallons)
  - Check anode rod every 3-5 years (replace if corroded)
  - Temperature: Set to 120°F for safety/efficiency
  - Lifespan: 10-15 years
- **Tankless:**
  - Descale annually (vinegar flush)
  - Filter cleaning
  - Lifespan: 15-20+ years

### Sump Pump Maintenance
- **Frequency:** Test quarterly (before rainy season)
- **Testing:** Pour water into sump pit - pump should activate
- **Battery Backup:** Check if present, test annually
- **Cleaning:** Remove debris annually
- **Replacement:** 5-7 years typical lifespan

### Pipe Inspection
- **Material Check:** PVC, copper, galvanized (age matters)
- **Polybutylene Pipes:** If installed 1978-1995, prone to failure - consider replacement
- **Freeze Protection:** Insulate exposed pipes in cold climates
- **Pressure:** Test annually (30-80 PSI normal)

## 3. Electrical System Maintenance

### Circuit Breaker Panel
- **Inspection:** Annually for tripped breakers, loose connections
- **Signs:** Burning smell, charred wires, frequent tripping
- **Panel Upgrade:** If > 40 years old or constantly tripping, professional inspection needed
- **NEVER:** Touch main breaker or exposed contacts

### Outlet & Switch Maintenance
- **Dead Outlets:** Check circuit breaker, GFCI reset
- **GFCI Outlets:** 
  - Test monthly (push test button)
  - Replace if won't reset
  - Required in bathrooms, kitchen, garage, outdoor areas
- **Loose Outlets:** Turn off breaker, tighten terminal screws
- **Cost:** $1-5 per outlet for DIY replacement

### Lighting Maintenance
- **LED Bulbs:** 
  - Lifespan: 25,000-50,000 hours (10-25 years)
  - Cost: Higher upfront, lower operating cost
  - Dimmer compatible: Check compatibility
- **CFL Bulbs:** 
  - Lifespan: 8,000-10,000 hours
  - Contains mercury - dispose properly
  - Energy efficient but longer warm-up time

### Panel Upgrade Signs
- **Need Upgrade if:**
  - 150-amp service (modern need 200-amp+)
  - Frequent breaker tripping
  - More than one split panel
  - Older wiring (aluminum)
- **Cost:** $1,500-3,000+
- **Must be:** Licensed electrician

### Ground Fault Circuit Interrupter (GFCI)
- **Location:** All wet areas (bathrooms, kitchen, garage)
- **Function:** Detects ground faults, cuts power within milliseconds
- **Test/Reset:** Buttons on outlet face
- **Lifespan:** 15-25 years (replace if won't reset)

## 4. Roof & Gutters

### Gutter Maintenance
- **Frequency:** Twice annually (spring, fall) - more if trees nearby
- **Cleaning:** Remove leaves, debris from gutters and downspouts
- **Inspection:** Look for sagging, separation, rust/holes
- **Repair:** Caulk small leaks, replace sections if necessary
- **Cost:** DIY ~$0, professional ~$150-300

### Downspout & Drainage
- **Extension:** Downspouts should extend 4-6 feet from foundation
- **Grading:** Ensure ground slopes away from house
- **Drainage:** Direct water away from basement/crawl space

### Roof Inspection
- **Frequency:** Annually (after storms, especially)
- **From Ground:** Look for missing/damaged shingles, moss, debris
- **Attic:** Check for leaks, water stains, mold
- **Professional Inspection:** Every 5-10 years

### Roof Repairs
- **Shingle Damage:** Individual shingles repairable ($2-5 per shingle)
- **Flashing Leaks:** Around chimneys, vents - seal with caulk
- **Moss/Algae:** Pressure wash (don't use harsh chemicals)
- **Roof Lifespan:** 
  - Asphalt shingles: 15-20 years
  - Metal: 40-70 years
  - Tile: 50+ years

### Ice Dam Prevention
- **Cause:** Heat escaping attic melts snow, refreezes at eave
- **Prevention:**
  - Improve attic insulation (R-38 to R-60)
  - Ventilate attic properly
  - Keep gutters clear
- **Treatment:** Heat cables (temporary fix only)

## 5. Basement & Foundation

### Water Intrusion Prevention
- **Inspection:** Look for cracks, efflorescence (white powder)
- **Grading:** Soil should slope away from foundation
- **Gutters:** Critical for diverting water (see Gutter Maintenance)
- **Sump Pump:** Install if water pooling occurs

### Basement Waterproofing
- **Interior:** Sealants, paint, puddle flanges
- **Exterior:** Grading, foundation coating, French drains
- **Cost:** $500-5,000+ depending on severity
- **Professional Assessment:** If significant moisture

### Foundation Crack Repair
- **Small Cracks (< 1/8 inch):** Cosmetic - caulk fill
- **Growing Cracks (> 1/4 inch):** Professional evaluation
- **Structural Issues:** Horizontal cracks, bowing - urgent professional attention

### Crawl Space Maintenance
- **Moisture Control:** Vapor barriers, dehumidifier if needed
- **Ventilation:** Check vents open in summer, close in winter
- **Insulation:** Pipes should be insulated in cold climates
- **Pest Prevention:** Seal gaps, trim vegetation away

## 6. Insulation & Weatherization

### Attic Insulation
- **R-Value Target:** 
  - Midwest: R-49 to R-60
  - Climate dependent (check local code)
- **Current Check:** Measure depth in several spots
- **Addition:** Can add blown-in insulation over existing
- **Cost:** $1-3 per square foot for blown-in
- **Payback:** 3-8 years in energy savings

### Weatherstripping & Caulking
- **Door Seals:** Replace worn weatherstripping ($5-20)
- **Window Seals:** Check for drafts, reseal if needed
- **Attic Access:** Weatherstrip and insulate hatch
- **Frequency:** Inspect annually, replace every 3-5 years

### Thermal Imaging
- **Purpose:** Identify heat loss areas
- **Professional Scan:** $100-300 (reveals insulation voids)
- **DIY:** Feel draft with hands near windows/doors

### Air Sealing
- **Priority Areas:** Attic penetrations, basement rim joists
- **Materials:** Caulk, spray foam, weatherstripping
- **Benefits:** 10-20% energy savings possible

## 7. Appliance Maintenance

### Refrigerator
- **Coil Cleaning:** Annually (back condenser coils)
- **Seal Check:** Close door on dollar bill - should resist pull
- **Drip Pan:** Clean quarterly (below fridge)
- **Filter:** Water/ice maker filter every 6 months
- **Lifespan:** 10-18 years

### Dishwasher
- **Filter Cleaning:** Before each load (remove debris)
- **Spray Arm:** Check for clogs in spray holes
- **Door Seal:** Inspect for cracks, replace if needed
- **Limescale:** Use rinse aid in hard water areas
- **Lifespan:** 9-10 years

### Washing Machine
- **Hose Inspection:** Check for cracks/bulges annually
- **Water Inlet Screens:** Clean if slow fill (remove hoses, clean filters)
- **Detergent Amount:** Use less in high-efficiency machines
- **Drain Line:** Keep clear, inspect connections
- **Lifespan:** 8-12 years

### Water Heater (See Plumbing Section)

### HVAC System (See HVAC Section)

### Oven/Range
- **Vent Hood:** Clean filter monthly (grease buildup)
- **Burner Maintenance:** Clean burner heads if clogged
- **Self-Clean Cycle:** Use 2-3 times yearly
- **Seal:** Check door seal integrity
- **Lifespan:** 13-15 years

## 8. Interior Maintenance

### Drywall Repairs
- **Small Holes (< 1 inch):** Spackling compound
- **Medium Holes (1-6 inch):** Self-adhesive mesh patch + compound
- **Large Holes (> 6 inch):** New drywall section needed
- **Moisture Damage:** Address underlying issue first

### Paint Maintenance
- **Interior:** Every 5-10 years (high-traffic areas more frequently)
- **Preparation:** Clean walls, patch holes, prime new areas
- **Quality:** Premium paint lasts longer (10+ years)
- **Color Fading:** UV exposure requires repainting sooner

### Flooring Maintenance
- **Wood:** Polish/refinish every 3-5 years, clean regularly
- **Laminate:** Avoid moisture, wipe spills immediately
- **Tile:** Grout resealing every 3-5 years
- **Carpet:** Professional cleaning annually, spot treatment

### Caulking & Sealing
- **Bathroom:** Re-caulk every 2-3 years or when gaps appear
- **Silicone:** Better for wet areas than acrylic
- **Baseboards:** Caulk to hide gaps, improve finished look

## 9. Door & Window Maintenance

### Door Hinges
- **Squeaking:** Lubricate with WD-40
- **Sagging:** Tighten hinge screws, may need shims
- **Replacement:** If severely damaged

### Window Sashes
- **Operation:** Should move smoothly
- **Balancing:** Check weights (if older windows)
- **Sash Cord:** Replace if frayed or broken
- **Lubrication:** Use wax, not oil

### Door Locks & Hardware
- **Lubrication:** Use graphite powder (not oil - attracts dirt)
- **Tightening:** Periodically tighten handle hardware
- **Replacement:** Locks wear over time (10-15 years typical)

### Storm Doors/Windows
- **Cleaning:** Screens annually, wash glass
- **Weatherstripping:** Replace if compressed/torn
- **Installation:** Remove in spring, install in fall (if seasonal)

## 10. Deck & Exterior Wood

### Wood Preservation
- **Cleaning:** Pressure wash annually (low PSI - don't damage wood)
- **Staining:** Every 2-3 years (prevents weathering, rot)
- **Sealant:** Protect from moisture and UV
- **Deck Boards:** Replace if soft, rotted, or severely split

### Deck Inspection
- **Fasteners:** Look for rust, corrosion, loose nails
- **Stairs:** Check for movement, wobble
- **Ledger Board:** Critical connection to house - check regularly
- **Footings:** Post should sit on concrete pier below frost line

### Pressure Washing Safety
- **PSI:** 3000 PSI max for wood (can cause damage)
- **Distance:** Keep nozzle 12+ inches from surface
- **Direction:** Spray with grain, not against
- **Safety:** Eye/ear protection required
