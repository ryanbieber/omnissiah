# House Maintenance Guide

Comprehensive home maintenance covering HVAC, plumbing, electrical, structural, and appliance systems.

## 1. HVAC System Maintenance

### Air Filter Replacement
- **Interval:** Every 1-3 months (more frequently in dusty environments)
- **Size:** Common sizes: 16x25x1, 16x25x4, 20x25x1 (check furnace)
- **MERV Rating:** 8-13 for residential (higher = more filtering, more restriction)
- **Cost:** $10-40 per filter
- **Procedure:**
  1. Turn off HVAC system
  2. Locate filter (usually near furnace or return air duct)
  3. Note arrow direction on frame
  4. Remove old filter, discard
  5. Insert new filter with arrow pointing toward furnace

### Annual HVAC Service
- **Timing:** Spring (AC prep) and Fall (heating prep)
- **Professional Service:** Annually by licensed technician
- **Tasks:**
  - Clean evaporator coil
  - Check refrigerant levels
  - Inspect blower components
  - Test electrical connections
  - Clean condenser coils
- **Cost:** $100-250 per visit

### Ductwork Inspection
- **Frequency:** Every 3-5 years
- **Signs:** Musty odors, uneven temperature, dust around vents
- **Cleaning:** Professional duct cleaning every 3-5 years
- **Sealing:** Use mastic sealant for leaks (not duct tape)

### Thermostat Programming
- **Heating Season:** Program lower temps when away/sleeping (68-70°F daytime)
- **Cooling Season:** Program higher temps when away (76-78°F daytime)
- **Savings:** ~1% per degree F per 8 hours of adjustment
- **Maintenance:** Replace batteries annually (spring forward, fall back)

## 2. Plumbing System Maintenance

### Leak Detection
- **Regular Inspection:** Check under sinks, around water heater, basement corners
- **Signs:** Water stains, musty smells, higher water bills
- **Quick Fix:** Tighten compression fittings (don't over-tighten)
- **Professional:** Call plumber if leak persists

### Faucet Maintenance
- **Aerator Cleaning:** Remove and clean annually (mineral buildup)
- **Dripping Faucet:** Usually just needs cartridge/washer replacement
- **Procedure:** Turn off water, remove handle, replace seal/cartridge
- **Cost:** $10-50 for parts vs. $100+ for professional

### Drain Maintenance
- **Prevention:** Use drain strainers, avoid pouring grease
- **Monthly:** Pour baking soda + vinegar + hot water (safe, effective)
- **Severe Clogs:** Use plumbing snake or call professional
- **Don't Use:** Harsh chemical drain cleaners

### Water Heater Care
- **Temperature:** Set to 120°F for safety and efficiency
- **Flushing:** Drain 5-10 gallons annually to remove sediment
- **Anode Rod:** Replace every 3-5 years (prevents rust)
- **Lifespan:** 10-15 years typical for tank-style

### Toilet Maintenance
- **Running Toilet:** Usually flapper valve - $15-30 repair kit
- **Leak Detection:** Food coloring in tank (should not appear in bowl)
- **Plunger Use:** Block overflow hole, use vigorous plunging
- **Professional:** Call if repeated clogs occur

## 3. Electrical System Maintenance

### Outlet & Switch Inspection
- **Safety Check:** Test outlets with circuit tester (under $10)
- **Spacing:** New outlets should be installed every 12 feet max
- **Covers:** Replace cracked or missing outlet covers
- **GFCI:** Install in bathrooms, kitchens, outdoor areas

### Circuit Breaker Panel
- **Annual Check:** Look for tripped breakers, signs of overheating
- **Temperature:** Should feel cool to touch
- **Labeling:** Keep panel clearly labeled
- **Professional:** Have electrician inspect if unsure

### Wiring Inspection
- **Visible Damage:** Check for fraying, exposed wires, burn marks
- **Update Needs:** Aluminum wiring in older homes may need upgrades
- **Safety:** Any damage = call licensed electrician immediately

## 4. Roof & Gutter Maintenance

### Roof Inspection
- **Frequency:** Annually, after major storms
- **Binoculars:** Can spot issues safely from ground level
- **Signs:** Missing shingles, granules in gutters, dark spots, leaks
- **Professional:** Have roofer inspect every 3-5 years

### Gutter Cleaning
- **Frequency:** Spring and Fall (or more in wooded areas)
- **Procedure:** Remove debris by hand, flush with water
- **Downspouts:** Ensure water drains 4-6 feet from foundation
- **Guards:** Consider gutter guards to reduce cleaning

### Caulking & Flashing
- **Inspection:** Check around chimneys, vents, roof penetrations
- **Caulking:** Replace dried or cracked caulk annually
- **Cost:** DIY caulk $5-10, professional sealing $200+

## 5. Foundation & Structural

### Foundation Inspection
- **Cracks:** Monitor existing cracks with mark and date
- **Growth:** Cracks > 1/4" wide or growing = call structural engineer
- **Moisture:** Efflorescence (white powder) indicates water seeping
- **Doors/Windows:** Sticking or not closing properly may indicate settling

### Basement Waterproofing
- **Drainage:** Ensure water slopes away from foundation
- **Sump Pump:** Test annually, replace battery in backup pump
- **Dehumidifier:** Use in humid climates to prevent mold
- **Sealing:** Use concrete sealant on cracks every 2-3 years

## 6. Appliance Maintenance

### Refrigerator
- **Coil Cleaning:** Vacuum coils under/behind every 3-6 months
- **Seal Check:** Door should seal tightly (dollar bill test)
- **Temperature:** Set fridge to 37-40°F, freezer to 0°F

### Washing Machine
- **Hose Inspection:** Check for cracks/leaks annually
- **Lint Trap:** Clean after every load for front-loaders
- **Hot Water:** Use cold water for most loads (saves energy)

### Dishwasher
- **Filter Cleaning:** Clean food trap before each load
- **Spray Arm:** Clear spray holes with toothpick
- **Rinse Aid:** Refill regularly for spot-free dishes

## 7. Seasonal Maintenance Checklist

### Spring
- [ ] Test HVAC before warm weather
- [ ] Inspect roof and gutters after winter
- [ ] Check basement for leaks
- [ ] Trim tree branches near roof
- [ ] Test sump pump

### Summer
- [ ] Replace HVAC filter
- [ ] Check exterior caulking
- [ ] Power wash deck/patio
- [ ] Inspect appliances

### Fall
- [ ] Clean gutters and downspouts
- [ ] Prepare HVAC for heating season
- [ ] Inspect weatherstripping
- [ ] Check attic ventilation

### Winter
- [ ] Monitor for ice dams
- [ ] Check for drafts around windows
- [ ] Verify pipes are insulated
- [ ] Test heating system

## 9. Home Maintenance Tips

- Keep all receipts and records for warranty/resale value
- Build a small repair fund for emergencies
- Learn basic troubleshooting for common issues
- Don't DIY electrical or structural work
- Get multiple quotes for major repairs
- Consider home warranty for coverage on appliances
- Take photos of issues for insurance purposes
