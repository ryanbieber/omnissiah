# Lawn Care Skills

## Region: Iowa (Waverly Area)
**USDA Hardiness Zone:** 4b  
**Growing Season:** Mid-May to Mid-October  
**Climate:** Cold winters, warm summers, moderate precipitation

---

## 1. Lawn Fertilization

### Optimal Schedule for Iowa
| Task | Month | Details |
|------|-------|---------|
| **Spring Fertilization** | April-May | Apply after grass greens up (80-100 lbs N/1000 sq ft annually) |
| **Early Summer** | June | Second application if using split schedule |
| **Late Summer/Early Fall** | August-September | Most important application - builds winter hardiness |
| **Fall Fertilization** | September-October | Apply before dormancy for spring green-up |

### Best Practices
- **Timing:** Apply when soil temp is 55-70°F consistently
- **Type:** Use slow-release nitrogen formulas for cool-season grasses
- **Frequency:** 2-4 applications per year
- **Amount:** 0.5-1 lb nitrogen per 1000 sq ft per application
- **Weather:** Apply before rain (within 48 hours) or water in after

### Recommended Fertilizers for Iowa Lawns
- Late Spring/Summer: 10-20-20 or 12-8-8 blend
- Fall: Higher potassium blend (e.g., 10-10-20) for winter strength
- Avoid heavy nitrogen in summer (causes disease stress)

## 2. Lawn Aeration

### Schedule
- **Primary Window:** April-May (spring aeration)
- **Secondary Window:** September-October (fall aeration)
- **Frequency:** Annually on compacted soil, biennially on healthy soil

### When to Aerate
- Soil compaction visible (water pooling, bare patches)
- Heavy clay soil
- After heavy foot traffic
- To prepare for overseeding

### Details
- **Equipment:** Core aerator (removes soil plugs)
- **Soil Condition:** Soil should be moist (not waterlogged)
- **Depth:** 2-3 inches
- **Spacing:** 1-2 inch plugs across lawn
- **Post-Aeration:** Leave plugs to break down naturally

---

## 3. Lawn Seeding & Overseeding

### Best Timing for Iowa
- **Primary:** August 15 - September 30 (optimal)
- **Secondary:** April 1 - May 15 (spring, less ideal)
- **Avoid:** June-July (too hot), November-March (too cold)

### Cool-Season Grass Mix for Waverly
- **Kentucky Bluegrass:** 40-50% (self-repairing, deep roots)
- **Perennial Ryegrass:** 30-40% (quick germination, fine texture)
- **Fescues:** 10-20% (shade tolerance, drought recovery)

### Seeding Guidelines
- **Soil Prep:** Aerate, rake bare spots, remove debris
- **Rate:** 5-6 lbs per 1000 sq ft (overseeding)
- **Moisture:** Keep soil moist for 2-3 weeks until germination
- **Traffic:** Avoid foot traffic until established (3-4 weeks)

## 4. Weed & Pest Management

### Seasonal Pest Schedule
| Season | Common Issues | Treatment |
|--------|---------------|-----------|
| **Spring (April-May)** | Winter dormancy wake-up, early weeds | Pre-emergent if skipped in fall |
| **Summer (June-Aug)** | Heat/drought stress, grub activity begins | Water deeply, monitor for damage |
| **Fall (Sept-Oct)** | Fall disease, grub larvae active | Preventive fungicide optional |
| **Winter** | Dormancy, snow mold risk | Good drainage, avoid salt damage |

### Weed Control Strategy
- **Pre-Emergent:** Early April (crabgrass prevention)
- **Post-Emergent:** Spot treat as needed (May-June, Sept-Oct)
- **Timing:** Apply when weeds are actively growing
- **Frequency:** As needed, max 2-3 applications/year

### Grub Management
- **Detection:** August-September monitoring
- **Treatment:** If >6 grubs per sq ft, treat in August-September
- **Preventive:** Diatomaceous earth or beneficial nematodes

## 5. Mowing Schedule

### Seasonal Guidelines
- **Spring (May):** Start when grass reaches 3.5-4 inches
- **Early Summer (June-July):** Weekly mowing, height 3-3.5 inches
- **Heat Stress Period (Mid-July-Aug):** Raise to 3.5-4 inches, mow 2x/week if needed
- **Fall (Sept-Oct):** Continue 3-3.5 inches until growth slows
- **Late Fall:** One final mow at 2.5 inches before dormancy

### Mowing Best Practices
- **Frequency:** No more than 1/3 blade removal per cut
- **Height:** Maintain 3-3.5 inches for optimal health
- **Blade Sharpness:** Sharpen every 8-10 hours of use
- **Pattern:** Alternate mowing patterns weekly to prevent rutting
- **Clippings:** Mulch (leave clippings for nitrogen return)

---

## 6. Thatch Management

### When to Dethatch
- Spring (April-May): If thatch > 0.5 inches
- Fall (Sept-Oct): For severe thatch buildup

### Thatch Removal
- **Equipment:** Dethatcher/vertical mower
- **Frequency:** Every 2-3 years if needed
- **Timing:** After spring green-up or before fall dormancy
- **Follow-up:** Overseed after dethatching

---

## 7. Dormancy & Winter Preparation

### September-October Tasks
- Final fertilization (potassium-rich)
- Aerate if needed
- Overseed bare spots
- Remove leaves (regular raking)
- Final mow at reduced height (2.5 inches)

### Winter Considerations
- Avoid salt damage (use calcium chloride alternative)
- Keep lawn off foot traffic when frozen/snow-covered
- Monitor for snow mold in spring

---

## 8. Spring Green-Up (April-May)

### Recovery Tasks
- Clean up winter debris and dead leaves
- First nitrogen application when grass actively growing
- Check for disease or winter damage
- Aerate if scheduled
- Pre-emergent crabgrass control early April
