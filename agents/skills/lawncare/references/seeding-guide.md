# Seeding and Overseeding Guide - Iowa/Waverly

## Best Timing for Iowa

- **Primary Window:** August 15 - September 30 (optimal)
- **Secondary Window:** April 1 - May 15 (spring, less ideal)
- **Avoid:** June-July (too hot), November-March (too cold)

## Cool-Season Grass Mix for Waverly

Recommended blend for Iowa Zone 4b:

- **Kentucky Bluegrass:** 40-50% (self-repairing, deep roots, drought tolerant)
- **Perennial Ryegrass:** 30-40% (quick germination, fine texture, traffic tolerance)
- **Fescues:** 10-20% (shade tolerance, drought recovery, fine leaf texture)

## Seeding Guidelines

### Pre-Seeding Preparation
1. Aerate lawn if compacted (2-3 inches deep)
2. Rake vigorously to remove debris and thatch
3. Apply pre-emergent (if winter weeds present)
4. Soil test for pH (ideal: 6.0-7.0) and nutrient levels
5. Level major low spots if present

### Seeding Rates
- **Full Renovation:** 6-8 lbs per 1000 sq ft
- **Overseeding:** 4-6 lbs per 1000 sq ft
- **Thin areas only:** 2-3 lbs per 1000 sq ft

### Moisture Management
1. **Pre-Seeding:** Soil should be moist but not waterlogged
2. **First 2 Weeks:** Keep soil consistently moist (like sponge, not soggy)
3. **Germination:** Requires daily light watering (¼-½ inch)
4. **Establishment:** 2-3 weeks before reducing watering frequency

### Traffic Restrictions
- **Foot Traffic:** Avoid completely for 3-4 weeks
- **Mowing:** Once grass reaches 3.5 inches, can mow
- **Equipment:** Keep mowers off area for 4-6 weeks

## Post-Seeding Timeline

| Week | Action | Notes |
|------|--------|-------|
| Week 1-2 | Light daily watering | Keep surface moist, germination phase |
| Week 3-4 | Reduce watering frequency | Established germination, deeper roots |
| Week 4-6 | First light mowing | Mow to 3-3.5 inches |
| Week 8+ | Normal maintenance | Regular schedule, full watering |

## Regional Considerations for Iowa

- **Spring Seeding:** Risk of hot, dry June-July
- **Fall Seeding:** Ideal - cool temps, adequate moisture, strong root development
- **Dormant Seeding:** Possible in November for spring germination
- **Frost Heave:** Avoid overseeding if frost heave likely (late March/April)
