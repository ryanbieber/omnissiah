# Car Maintenance Skills

## Overview
Professional-grade vehicle maintenance scheduling and execution across all vehicle systems.

---

## 1. Oil Changes

### Standard Intervals
- **Conventional Oil:** Every 3,000-5,000 miles or 3-6 months
- **Synthetic Oil:** Every 7,500-10,000 miles or 6-12 months
- **Synthetic Blend:** Every 5,000-7,500 miles or 6 months

### Pre-Oil Change Checklist
- Engine at operating temperature (warm but not hot)
- Vehicle on level ground
- Drain plug accessible and clear

### Oil Change Procedure
1. Drain old oil into drain pan
2. Replace drain plug (replace sealing washer)
3. Locate and replace oil filter
4. Fill with new oil to level indicated on dipstick
5. Check for leaks around drain plug and filter
6. Reset oil change indicator light

### Post-Service
- Document odometer reading
- Record oil type and filter model
- Schedule next change

## 2. Air Filter Replacement

### Engine Air Filter
- **Interval:** Every 12,000-15,000 miles or annually
- **Signs:** Reduced fuel economy, weak acceleration, dirty appearance
- **Location:** Top or side of engine (varies by vehicle)
- **Replacement:** Simple snap or bolt clips

### Cabin Air Filter
- **Interval:** Every 15,000-30,000 miles or annually
- **Signs:** Weak air flow, musty odors
- **Location:** Behind glove box or under dash
- **Maintenance:** Vacuum first, replace if damaged

---

## 3. Tire Maintenance

### Tire Rotation
- **Interval:** Every 5,000-8,000 miles or with oil changes
- **Pattern:** Front-to-back or cross-pattern (see manual)
- **Benefits:** Even wear, extended tire life

### Tire Pressure
- **Check Frequency:** Monthly + before long trips
- **Correct Pressure:** See door jamb or fuel door sticker
- **Conditions:** Check when tires are cold (before driving)
- **Seasonal:** Adjust for temperature changes

### Tire Replacement
- **Tread Depth:** Replace at 2/32 inch (use penny test)
- **Warranty:** Typically 3-5 years depending on brand
- **Signs:** Uneven wear, bulges, cracks, visible belts

### Wheel Alignment
- **When:** After hitting potholes, new tires, pulling to one side
- **Interval:** Every 2-3 years as preventive measure
- **Cost:** $100-200 for 4-wheel alignment

---

## 4. Battery Maintenance

### Battery Life
- **Lifespan:** 3-5 years typical
- **Climate Impact:** Extreme heat/cold reduces life

### Battery Check
- **Frequency:** Annually (especially before winter)
- **Corrosion:** Clean white/blue corrosion with baking soda solution
- **Connections:** Ensure tight connections, no fraying
- **Charging:** Alternator test at service intervals

### Battery Replacement
- **Signs:** Slow cranking, clicking sounds, dim lights
- **Procedure:** Negative (-) terminal first, then positive (+)
- **New Battery:** Get matched amperage for vehicle

## 5. Brake Maintenance

### Brake Pad Inspection
- **Interval:** Every 10,000-15,000 miles or annually
- **Signs:** Squealing, reduced responsiveness, dashboard warning
- **Thickness:** Replace when < 3mm remaining

### Brake Fluid
- **Interval:** Every 2 years or 24,000 miles
- **Color Check:** Should be clear amber (brown = replacement time)
- **Moisture:** Absorbs moisture over time - regular replacement prevents corrosion

### Brake Disc/Rotor Inspection
- **Condition:** Check for scoring, warping
- **Replacement:** When pads are replaced if damaged
- **Cost:** Plan for replacement every 50,000-70,000 miles

### Anti-Lock Brake System (ABS)
- **Warning Light:** Have diagnostic performed immediately
- **Service:** May require specialized equipment

---

## 6. Fluid Checks & Changes

### Transmission Fluid
- **Interval:** Every 30,000-60,000 miles (varies by type)
- **Color:** Red/pink (brown or burnt smell = replacement needed)
- **Level:** Check with engine running, on level ground
- **ATF vs. Manual:** Different fluids - use correct type

### Coolant/Antifreeze
- **Interval:** Every 30,000 miles or 2-3 years
- **Type:** Red, orange, green, pink (vary by manufacturer)
- **System Flush:** Drain, flush, refill to prevent contamination
- **Concentration:** 50/50 mix with water

### Brake Fluid
- **Interval:** Every 2 years (see Brake Maintenance)
- **Type:** DOT 3, 4, or 5.1 (never mix)
- **Cost:** ~$100-150 for full system bleed

### Power Steering Fluid
- **Interval:** Every 40,000-50,000 miles
- **Symptoms:** Difficulty steering, grinding noise, leaks
- **Color:** Pink, red, or amber depending on type

## 7. Belt & Hose Inspection

### Serpentine/Drive Belts
- **Interval:** Inspect every 20,000 miles, replace 40,000-80,000 miles
- **Signs:** Cracks, fraying, squealing, poor performance
- **Cost:** $75-150 per belt
- **Related:** Tensioner replacement common with belt

### Hoses
- **Inspection:** Look for leaks, cracks, bulges, soft spots
- **Replacement:** Every 4-6 years or 40,000-60,000 miles
- **Cost:** $20-100 per hose depending on location

---

## 8. Spark Plugs

### Replacement Intervals
- **Standard Spark Plugs:** Every 30,000 miles
- **Platinum Spark Plugs:** Every 100,000 miles
- **Iridium Spark Plugs:** Every 100,000-120,000 miles

### Symptoms of Bad Plugs
- Rough idling
- Poor fuel economy
- Difficulty starting
- Engine knocking/pinging

### Procedure
- Remove old plugs (one at a time, note positions)
- Gap new plugs to specification (usually 0.028-0.044 inch)
- Install new plugs with proper torque
- Connect spark plug wires/coils in correct order

---

## 9. Engine Air Intake System

### Clean vs. Replace Air Filter
- **Cleaning:** Light dust - tap or gently vacuum
- **Replacement:** Heavy dirt, oil contamination, damage
- **Interval:** Check every 5,000 miles, replace 12,000-15,000 miles

### Fuel Filter
- **Interval:** Every 20,000-30,000 miles
- **Signs:** Hard starting, hesitation, reduced fuel efficiency
- **Location:** Inline or module (fuel tank) - varies by vehicle

---

## 10. Suspension & Steering

### Shock Absorber/Strut Inspection
- **Interval:** Every 50,000 miles or annually
- **Signs:** Bouncing, poor handling, uneven tire wear
- **Lifespan:** 50,000-100,000 miles typical

### Undercarriage Inspection
- **Frequency:** Annually or after rough road use
- **Check:** Rust, loose components, leaks
- **Cost:** Often included in routine service

### Wheel Bearing Maintenance
- **Symptoms:** Grinding, clicking while turning, vibration
- **Replacement:** Professional service required
- **Interval:** 150,000+ miles or as needed

---

## 11. Windshield & Wipers

### Wiper Blade Maintenance
- **Replacement:** Every 6-12 months or when streaking/chattering
- **Inspection:** Check for splits, hardening, uneven pressure
- **Cost:** $15-40 per blade

### Windshield
- **Damage:** Small chips repairable, cracks require replacement
- **Replacement:** $200-500+ depending on vehicle
- **Maintenance:** Keep clean, use quality washer fluid

---

## 12. Battery Test

### Digital Battery Tester
- **Load Test:** Indicates battery health
- **CCA (Cold Cranking Amps):** Should be within spec
- **Voltage:** 12.6+ volts when engine off

### When to Test
- Before winter (August-September)
- If experiencing starting issues
- Every 2-3 years preventively

## Seasonal Maintenance Calendar

| Season | Tasks |
|--------|-------|
| **Spring** | Battery test (winter wear), Fluid top-offs, Tire inspection |
| **Summer** | AC service, Cooling system check, Tire pressure (heat expansion) |
| **Fall** | Battery load test, Antifreeze flush check, Wiper blades, Tire tread |
| **Winter** | Battery maintenance, Tire chains/snow tires, Fluid viscosity check |
